You’ve likely heard the commonly stated guideline that your budget should equal about two months’ salary. This was actually originated from an advertising firm in the 1940s. We believe every couple is different and there shouldn’t be any rules or guidelines on the budget you set. What’s important is that you are comfortable with the budget. Read How to Set an Engagement Ring Budget for more tips.

To choose the perfect ring, consider what fits your partner's lifestyle and personality best. In addition to your budget, your partner's personal style and daily activities are the main considerations that should guide your selection of a setting. Read How to Choose an Engagement Ring for more tips.

Despite the terms being used somewhat interchangeably, each ring signifies different milestones. There are a few differences between an engagement ring and a wedding ring, but the main difference between the two is when each ring is given. An engagement ring is typically given at the time of a proposal and a wedding ring is commonly given when a couple of exchanges marriage vows. Read The Right Way to Wear a Wedding Ring to learn more.
